Capital One Credit Card Pre-Approval Experience

As the Senior Manager of the Acquisition Experience Design team, I led a team of UX, UI, and Content designers and collaborated with Tech, Product, and Marketing partners to deliver strong business results in credit card Acquisitions and transform the user experience with human-centered design.

The Problem

The existing Pre-Qualification tool at Capital One was not user-centered, leading to a low conversion rate and inaccurate determinations of credit eligibility. The current tool was also considered a marketing tool rather than an application; therefore its product evolution was reactive more than strategic, and the front-end was built on a different platform than the credit decisioning capability, which created technical and business complexities.

My Role

As Senior Manager of Experience Design, I led a cross-functional team of designers and partnered with Product, Business, Marketing and Tech to define the roadmap and priorities, align teams to shifting business goals, and deliver experiences to market for regular testing and optimization. I drove a Product Vision to enable a customer-centered application experience and generate revenue for the business.

Partnering & Adding Value

Overview

As a design team leader, I emphasized the importance of providing immediate value and impact through partnering for quick wins while getting embedded into the Pre-Approval space. I led discussions with partners about applying the design standards set forth by the Enterprise design system, and I guided my team to create scalable designs that enabled Tech to deliver more efficiently while allowing Design to allocate more time to impactful designs.

Driving User Research

Research Approach

I led my team in developing hypotheses, prototypes and a testing plan for a user research study. I engaged Research leads to support on the study, and I collaborated with key stakeholders by discussing research objectives and sharing artifacts like the research brief, prototypes, and the discussion guide for continuous input.

The research enabled us to test our hypotheses about simplifying the shopping and application experience in order to learn and represent users' needs with our partners when making experience decisions.

We tested 4 prototypes with 3 variables with the goal of understanding how and when people think about eligibility in their credit card shopping journey.

Application Format

Order of Questions

Outcomes
  • Happy path (approval)

We tested whether participants liked Pre-Approval separate or combined with a Credit Card Application

We tested variations of user flow and task sequencing to learn whether participants prefer a single-page or multi-page experience

We tested outcome page variations to learn what participants like and dislike about different outcome experiences (happy / sad path, upsell / downsell)

Research Insights & Design Strategy

Synthesis

Following the research, I led my Design team in research synthesis over the course of two weeks. I guided the team to consolidate the insights into a presentable format for our partners and stakeholders by providing the format and outline of the deck. I regularly reviewed the document and provided feedback to my team through individual conversations and group forums.

I created spaces to share research learnings with key stakeholders, and I emphasized big opportunities to improve the user experience that could meaningfully impact our key Acquisition success metrics.

I developed a proposal for the next steps which included bringing teams together to brainstorm opportunities based on our findings. I collaborated with business analysts to scope the highest-leverage opportunities from the brainstorm, and I gained partner buy-in to create and manage a Design-driven backlog for further Product development.

Human-Centered Design — 4 Experiments

My UX design team utilized a human-centered approach, leveraging insights from rigorous user research and synthesis to inform our ideation, iteration, and development processes. Through this approach, we were able to design and produce product tests that addressed the needs and pain points of our target users while also meeting key business objectives. I guided the work by leading weekly design critiques, stakeholder reviews, and regular engineering discussions and collaboration.

Experiment 01

Celebratory Moment in the Application Journey

Hypothesis

Celebrating the moment an applicant receives a pre-approved offer will create excitement, build customer engagement and lead to more people continuing on to apply for a card.

Result

Increased Net Present Value (NPV), increase in annual incremental New Accounts Booked (NABs) and reduced drop-off between Pre-Approval and the Application.

Experiment 02

Comparison Offer Page

Hypothesis

Enabling applicants to easily compare features of the cards they're approved for and offering them guidance through tags and filters will help them feel confident that they're making the right choice and lead to higher acceptance rates.

Result

Increased Net Present Value (NPV), increase in annual incremental New Accounts Booked (NABs) and reduced drop-off between Pre-Approval and the Application.

Experiment 03

1-2-3 Application Process Stepper

Hypothesis

Giving users more context about the Pre-Approval process, the steps, and the benefit for them will increase form submission and conversion.

Result

Increased Net Present Value (NPV) and increase in annual incremental New Accounts Booked (NABs).

Experiment 04

Pre-Approval and Shortened Credit Card Application

Hypothesis

Condensing the Pre-Approval and Application processes into a single flow will reduce drop-off on the Application.

Result

Decreased drop-off, increased NPV, and increased annual incremental NABs.

Design Leadership & Team Enablement
01

Establishing a Successful Working Model

As a Design Leader, I established a successful working model for the Experience Design team that demonstrated how design can work ahead of delivery. This required building partnerships and trust with Product and Tech partners, and educating them on the need for design to have sufficient time to iterate and test hypotheses. We faced challenges with reactive timelines and independent requests from multiple Product partners, which strained communication and impacted overall delivery.

To overcome these challenges, I discussed an ideal working model with my design team and illustrated a working model that adapted a dual-track scrum methodology, resulting in a much closer relationship with Product and Tech and both business segments. I created opportunities for members of my team to present and socialize the proposal with various partners.

02

Leading Stakeholder Discussions & Priority Alignment

As a senior design manager, I am highly effective in guiding my team towards producing impactful results. In response to the challenges posed by an overwhelming volume of haphazard design support requests, I secured buy-in from Product and Tech partners to establish a structured backlog. To ground our design efforts in data, I facilitated a cross-functional brainstorm with key business stakeholders and partners, during which my team collected existing business problems and customer problems.

Through a rigorous prioritization exercise, I led a core team of stakeholders in defining the team's near-term deliverables. In collaboration with the Experience Design team, we synthesized all the ideas generated during the brainstorming session and categorized them into themes, building out hypotheses and prioritizing them into a shared backlog.

03

Developing a Design-Owned Backlog

As a result of this work, I led a successful design-led effort to organize intent with our partners, which helped to define priorities and keep our team organized while managing our workload. The process of involving partners in the brainstorming session ensured that they felt included in the design process, which led to a more collaborative and effective outcome.

My team was also empowered to see what we were working towards, which led to increased motivation and a shared sense of purpose. These results are a testament to the effectiveness of my design leadership and the positive impact that it can have on the design process and the broader organization.

04

Creating a Comprehensive Design Repository

One of the most rewarding outcomes of our team's work was the creation of a comprehensive design repository that helped us scale rapidly and maintain a clear, evolving story. As the team and business intent grew, it became increasingly important to have a centralized repository where we could store our work, including designs, hypotheses, research, sprints, test results, and business outcomes.

Drawing on my expertise as a Senior Design Manager, I guided my team in creating a timeline of deliverables that our Experience Design team had produced, which has since become a critical tool for onboarding new designers and business team members. By prioritizing collaboration and organization, we were able to create a valuable resource that continues to drive success for our team and the broader organization.

Conclusion

By centering the user in every design decision and enabling my team to lead with research, strategy, and partnership, we delivered measurable improvements to Capital One's credit card acquisition experience.
